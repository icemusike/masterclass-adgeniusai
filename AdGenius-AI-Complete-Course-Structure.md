### Lesson 8.2: Performance-Based Pricing (Advanced) (15 min)
**RISK-REVERSAL STRATEGY:**
Instead of flat monthly fee, offer:
- Low monthly retainer ($200) + % of ad spend (3-5%)
- OR low monthly retainer ($300) + % of revenue generated

**EXAMPLE:**
"I'll charge you $300/month + 4% of your ad spend. If you're spending $5,000/month on ads, that's $200 more = $500 total. But if your ad spend grows to $10,000, I make $700 total because I helped you scale."

**PROS:**
- Easier to close clients (lower upfront risk)
- Potentially higher earnings long-term
- Incentive alignment (you win when they win)

**CONS:**
- You need access to their ad account (trust issue)
- More complex billing
- Income less predictable

**WHEN TO USE THIS:**
- Large clients with big budgets
- You have proven results
- They're hesitant about flat fees

**WHITE-LABEL PRICING:**

- **Monthly retainer:** $2,000-3,000/month for unlimited clients

**BENEFITS:**

## BONUS MODULE 3: TROUBLESHOOTING & FAQ (30 minutes)

**Common problems and solutions:**

### FAQ 3.1: "I'm not getting any responses to my outreach" (8 min)
**POSSIBLE ISSUES:**
- Your subject lines are too generic
- You're not personalizing enough
- You're not including samples
- You're reaching out to the wrong prospects
- Your emails are going to spam

**SOLUTIONS:**
- Test different subject lines
- Spend 2 minutes researching each prospect before emailing
- Always attach 3-5 custom samples
- Focus on businesses actively running ads
- Use a warm-up service for your email (Mailwarm, Lemwarm)

### FAQ 3.2: "Clients are asking for revisions beyond scope" (6 min)
**SOLUTION:**
- Set clear revision policy upfront: "2 rounds of revisions included"
- After that, charge: "$50 per additional revision" or "Unlimited revisions for +$100/month"
- Be firm but professional: "Happy to make these changes! That would be outside our agreement, so I'd need to charge $X for the additional work."

### FAQ 3.3: "A client isn't paying on time" (5 min)
**SOLUTION:**
- Automated billing prevents this (Stripe auto-charge)
- If manual: Send reminder 3 days before due date
- If late: Pause service until paid
- Professional message: "Hi [Name], just noticed the payment for this month didn't go through. I've paused your account until we get that sorted. Let me know if you need help!"

### FAQ 3.4: "Client wants to cancel" (8 min)
**SOLUTION:**
- Ask why: "Can I ask what prompted this decision?"
- Common reasons: Budget, not seeing results, found someone cheaper
- Offer solution: Lower tier, payment plan, discount, pause instead of cancel
- Exit gracefully: "I understand. No problem! If anything changes in the future, feel free to reach out."
- Ask for referral: "Before you go, do you know anyone who might benefit from this service?"

### FAQ 3.5: "I'm stuck at 5 clients and can't scale" (3 min)
**POSSIBLE ISSUES:**
- Not spending enough time on outreach
- Outreach methods not working
- Positioning issue (price too high, service unclear)

**SOLUTIONS:**
- Block 5 hours/week specifically for new client acquisition
- Try different outreach methods (email, phone, LinkedIn, referrals)
- Get testimonials from happy clients and feature them prominently
